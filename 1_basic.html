Ada Lovela

Ada Lovelace (1815-1852) was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Her notes on the engine include what is recognized as the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine, and she is often regarded as the first computer programmer.

Timeline

  • 1815: Born in London, England
  • 1833: Started working with Charles Babbage on his Difference Engine
  • 1843: Published her notes on Babbage's Analytical Engine, including the first algorithm for processing by a machine
  • 1852: Died in London, England
